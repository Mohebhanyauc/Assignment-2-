My interpritation to this graph is that as the income increases the cases increases not because the high income people actually get infected more often, but because they test more as they care more about their health and are more willing to pay to make sure they are in good health. For example, if someone was coughing and was feeling sick, the probability that he would test if he was in the high income group would be higher that if a low income earner would test. Clearly because he is more eilling to pay and not because he is more likely to actually get infected.

Conclusion:¶

Low income group stayed low in both years because they dont really have the money to test and report results to the government.On the other hand, lower middle income group decreases from 3000 to 1000. This decrease could be either because they were more willing to pay for testing in 2020 than in 2021, because in 2020: virus was unclear; covid-19 was deadly; no medications or cure was available; everyone was helpless and afraid. IN other words, I believe in 2020 they had to pay even if it was costly for all groups except the low income. So, all these aspects could have contribulted to this major decrease in the lower middle income and the minor decrease in the higher middle income.
